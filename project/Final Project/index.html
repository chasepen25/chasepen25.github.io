What Makes A Great Movie For Me

A great movie, to me, is one that keeps working on you even after it ends.

A good movie, for me, makes me think. If a film really gets me thinking, it turns into one of the most rewarding experiences I can have. Some of my favorite movies are the ones that stay with me long after they are over, and sometimes I will be thinking about them for weeks.

A few examples are Interstellar, The Pursuit of Happyness, and The Life of Chuck. Each of these films pushes you to reflect and consider deeper ideas about life, time, and personal meaning. I still remember watching Interstellar for the first time and continuing to think about it for months afterward. In fact, that movie is what made me love film and storytelling in general.

There are some fantastic movies that you watch and they give you a great afternoon. But the best movies are the ones where, after the credits roll, that is when the real amazing part starts.

In my opinion, the worst thing a movie can be is non-thought-provoking and boring. Those two combined make a bad movie. There are some movies that are good for a one-night watch, and that is okay. But if I am bored while watching a movie and I do not think about it after, then I do not think it was the greatest thing.
